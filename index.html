About

Hi, I'm a postdoctoral researcher in computer science in the imec-DistriNet research group at KU Leuven, Belgium. I currently work at the crossroads of low-power embedded architectures, long range wireless transmissions, network routing protocols and distributed systems. These days I'm involved in the first distributed open federation for LoRaWAN networks: FLIP.

I received my PhD in Computer Science in 2017 from Université de Rennes 1 supervised by Pr. François TAÏANI and I'm now pursuing a postdoc at the KU Leuven in the Networked Embedded Software task force led by Pr. Danny Hughes.

Prior to that I received my engineering degree in electronics from Université Toulouse III and CESI before working for 8 years in the industry. During this time spent in research & development teams I have been involved in many different projects, most notably in the design of optical backbone equipments for people handling a lot of packets, I also worked on NATO/Red Cross commissioned equipments embedded onboard flying machines and designed embedded wireless telemetry for scientists flying stratospheric balloons.
For more detailed infos, my resume is available here.

Research interests

  • Ultra low-power embedded architectures
  • Wireless sensor networks
  • Self-organizing distributed architectures
  • Collaborative approaches on information diffusion
  • Self-stabilizing meshed networks
